Since 2011, schema.org, introduced by the major search engines Bing, Google, Yahoo! and Yandex, became a de facto standard for annotating and publishing structured data on the web. Schema.org focuses on defining the item types and properties to describe “things” on web pages that are most valuable to automated agents (e.g. search engines, chatbots or personal assistant systems). This means search engines and dialog assistants get the structured information they need most to improve search results and give the required answer. The usage of schema.org gives a motivation to focus on a certain domain for improving vocabulary and providing domain-specific patterns for semantic annotations.

A Domain Specification is a domain-specific pattern for semantic annotation of web content, which consists of types, properties and ranges [Şimşek et al., 2017]. It is based on schema.org vocabulary, but also extends subsets of it to meet certain tasks and domains. The goal of Domain Specifications is to represent a selected collection of types, properties and their ranges needed to semantically annotate a selected domain (e.g. hotel, restaurant, ski resort) [Panasiuk et al., 2018a], [Panasiuk et al., 2018b]. Domain Specifications can be seen as a recommended standard for annotating web content.
To build a collection of Domain Specifications for the tourism domain we analyzed the following data sources, including their websites and databases, taking into account Google Search Feature Gallery recommendations: (1) data of destination marketing organizations, such as Best of Zillertal Fügen, Mayrhofen Tourism Association, Seefeld Tourism Association, and Zillertal Arena, described in [Akbar et al., 2017]; (2) Interactive maps of General Solution, described in [Panasiuk et al., 2018b]; (3) Ski school Arosa; (4) Outdooractive; (5) Intermaps; (6) Tourism related services from the destination management system of Feratel; (7) Accommodation data of Brandenburg provided by Tomas; (8) Tirol Werbung. This data covers a wide variety of information topics including events, accommodation and accommodation offers, food establishments, ski areas, sports activity locations, the region, press release articles, the destination management organization itself and a large variety of infrastructure information.
